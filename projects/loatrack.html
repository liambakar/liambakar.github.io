Exploring Novel Food Tracking Techniques

In progress

Context

In recent years, nutrition has taken center stage in public health conversations, with approximately 40% of adults actively trying to lose weight . At the same time, 2-4% of young adults struggle with clinically diagnosed eating disorders, underscoring the fine line between health-conscious behavior and disordered eating. Eating behavior is further complicated by the fact that food is not only essential for survival but also deeply embedded in cultural identity and social relationships, meaning individuals often lack complete control over what, when, and how they eat. Nutritional tracking tools emerged as a way to bring more awareness and structure to eating habits. The 69% of U.S. adults keeping track of at least one health indicator (weight, diet, exercise regimen, or symptom) can now monitor intake, set goals, and reflect on patterns using modern logging techniques. However, the effectiveness of these tools depends on factors such as usability, psychological impact, long-term sustainability, and the risk of tracking fatigue, a common phenomenon in which users disengage due to the effort and mental load of constant self-monitoring.

Proposed Solution

To balance the tradeoff between time-consuming and inconvenient, manual tracking and innacurate, automated tracking, I propose using LLMs and a series of AI context and techniques to improve nutrition tracking. This allows users to log in a modality of their choosing (image, text, audio) without sacrificing the accuracy.

The project flow is as follows:

  • The user inputs a free-form log of an unconstrained modality (image, text, audio)
  • We use OpenAI's CLIP model to extract the input embeddings and use RAG to provide context to the model
  • We provide the user's previously purchased ingredients as context
  • We allow the model to ask follow up questions to the users to gain a wholistic understanding

With all this information, and a multimodal instance of Gemini, we are able to generate more comprehensive and accurate food logs without requiring the user to do much work.
